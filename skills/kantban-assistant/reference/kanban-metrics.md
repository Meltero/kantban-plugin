# Kanban Metrics Reference

This file explains the metrics that KantBan tracks and how Claude should communicate them to users. The goal is clarity, not precision — users need to make decisions, not write papers.

---

## Core Flow Metrics

### Cycle Time

**What it is:** The time from when work starts (ticket moves to In Progress) to when it's done (ticket moves to Done).

**Why it matters:** Shorter cycle times mean faster feedback loops and more predictable delivery. High cycle time variance is often more of a problem than high average cycle time.

**How to communicate it:**
- "Your average cycle time this week is 1.4 days. That's down from 2.1 days last week — the board is flowing well."
- "This ticket has been In Progress for 6 days. The team average is 1.5 days — this one might be stuck."

**What drives cycle time up:** Large tickets, unclear scope, blocked work, multitasking, waiting for review.

---

### WIP (Work In Progress) Limits

**What it is:** A cap on how many tickets can be In Progress at once in a column or across the board.

**Why it matters:** WIP limits force the team to finish work before starting new work. They prevent the illusion of productivity (lots of things "in progress") while hiding the reality (nothing actually finishing).

**Little's Law:** `Cycle Time = WIP ÷ Throughput`
- If throughput stays constant and WIP increases, cycle time increases proportionally.
- Counterintuitive: doing less at once makes things finish faster.

**How to communicate it:**
- "You have 7 tickets In Progress but a WIP limit of 3. That explains why cycle times are up — consider finishing some before starting more."
- "WIP limit hit on the Review column. Nothing can move there until a ticket is completed or moved."

**KantBan enforcement:** WIP limits are checked by `kantban_check_transition`. If a transition would exceed a WIP limit, the tool returns a warning. Claude should surface this, not hide it.

---

### Throughput

**What it is:** The number of tickets completed per time period (usually per week or per sprint).

**Why it matters:** Throughput is the most reliable measure of team output. It's more stable than velocity (which can be inflated by generous story point estimates).

**How to communicate it:**
- "You completed 12 tickets last week, up from 8 the week before."
- "Throughput has been declining for 3 weeks. That's worth investigating."

**Use `kantban_get_velocity` to fetch throughput trends.**

---

### Work Item Age

**What it is:** How long a ticket has been in its current column without moving.

**Why it matters:** Old items in non-Done columns are often forgotten, blocked, or deprioritized. They're a leading indicator of problems.

**How to communicate it:**
- "PROJ-18 has been In Review for 9 days. Is it waiting on something?"
- "You have 4 tickets in Backlog that haven't moved in 30+ days. Want to groom those?"

**KantBan surfaces Work Item Age in `kantban_detect_bottlenecks` and the health resource.**

## Forecasting

### Monte Carlo Forecasting

**What it is:** A probabilistic forecast based on historical throughput. Instead of estimating future work directly, it simulates thousands of possible futures using past data and returns a probability distribution.

**Why it matters:** It's more honest than point estimates. Instead of "we'll ship in 3 weeks," it says "there's an 85% chance of shipping within 5 weeks."

**How `kantban_forecast_completion` works:**
- Takes the count of remaining tickets and historical throughput.
- Runs Monte Carlo simulations.
- Returns a probability distribution: "50% by [date], 85% by [date], 95% by [date]."

**How to communicate forecasts:**
- Lead with the 85% confidence date, not the 50%.
- "Based on your current velocity, there's an 85% chance this sprint completes by Friday. The 50% date is Wednesday."
- Never say "it will be done by X" — say "there's an N% chance of completing by X."
- If the forecast is wide (e.g., 50% = 2 weeks, 95% = 8 weeks), that's a signal of high variance — mention it.

**Use `kantban_forecast_completion` to generate these numbers.** Don't estimate manually.

---

### Ticket Estimation

**What it is:** A complexity estimate for a single ticket, generated by `kantban_estimate_ticket`.

**How it works:** The tool uses ticket title, description, and acceptance criteria — combined with similar historical tickets — to suggest a size (XS/S/M/L/XL or time estimate).

**How to communicate it:**
- "Based on similar past tickets, this looks like a Medium (~2-4 hours). Does that seem right?"
- If the estimate seems off to the user, trust their intuition — they know the codebase.

**Don't over-rely on estimates.** They're hints, not commitments. Cycle time data is more reliable for forecasting than up-front estimates.

## suggest_next_task Scoring

The `kantban_suggest_next_task` tool uses a scoring algorithm to rank available tickets. Understanding it helps Claude explain recommendations:

**Scoring factors (approximate weights):**
1. **Priority** — High priority tickets score higher.
2. **Age** — Tickets that have been waiting longer score higher (avoids starvation).
3. **Dependencies** — Tickets that unblock other tickets score higher.
4. **WIP balance** — Tickets that move toward WIP limit targets score higher.
5. **Context switching cost** — Tickets related to recent work score higher (reduces cognitive load).

**How to communicate a recommendation:**
- "I'm suggesting PROJ-24 because it's high priority, has been waiting 3 days, and would unblock PROJ-25 and PROJ-26."
- "PROJ-18 is next because you were just working on auth — continuing in the same area reduces context switching."

Be specific about the reason. "The algorithm ranked it first" is not useful.

## Retrospective Insights

`kantban_retrospective_insights` analyzes completed work over a time period and returns patterns. Claude should translate these into actionable language:

| Metric Finding | What to Say |
|---|---|
| Cycle time trending up | "Work items are taking longer to complete than they were 4 weeks ago. WIP has also increased — they're likely related." |
| High variance in cycle time | "Completion times are unpredictable — some tickets take 2 hours, others take 2 weeks. That suggests unclear scope or hidden blockers." |
| Throughput decline | "Fewer tickets are completing each week. Is the team taking on larger work, or are there more blockers than usual?" |
| Many re-opened tickets | "Several tickets were moved back from Done. That usually means acceptance criteria weren't clear at the start." |
| Bottleneck column | "The Review column is a consistent bottleneck — items spend 3x longer there than anywhere else." |

Always suggest one concrete action alongside the observation. Metrics without action are just noise.
